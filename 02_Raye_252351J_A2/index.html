What is Volleyball?

Volleyball is a dynamic team sport played by two teams of six players on a rectangular court divided by a net. The main objective is to send the ball over the net and land it in the opponent's court, while preventing the opponent from doing the same.

Each team is allowed up to three touches to return the ball, typically consisting of a bump (pass), set, and spike (attack). This creates a fast-paced, strategic game that requires excellent teamwork and communication.

History & Origins

Volleyball was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan in Holyoke, Massachusetts, United States. Originally called "Mintonette," it was designed as a blend of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball.

Morgan created the game for businessmen who found basketball too vigorous. Since its humble beginnings, volleyball has grown into a global phenomenon ,played indoors and on beaches, with professional leagues worldwide and Olympic competition since 1964.

Why Play Volleyball?

The game emphasizes teamwork, agility, coordination, and communication, making it both physically challenging and mentally engaging. Unlike many sports, volleyball requires every player to be involved in both offense and defense.

Whether you're spiking at the net, diving for a dig, or setting up the perfect play, volleyball offers an exciting blend of athleticism and strategy that makes it thrilling to both play and watch.

Basic Objective

Two teams (usually 6 players each) compete to score points by hitting the ball over the net and into the opposing team's court. A team scores when the opposing team: Lets the ball land on their court. Hits the ball out of bounds. Fails to return the ball properly within 3 touches.

Gameplay Rules

Three touches max per side: The ball can only be hit up to three times before it must go over the net. Same player cannot hit twice in a row. Legal hits include bump (forearm), set (fingertips), and spike (overhand attack). No catching or holding the ball. The ball must not touch the ground on your side.

Faults (Mistakes)

You lose the rally if: Ball lands outside the court. Ball hits the antenna (stick on the net). Player touches the net. Player crosses the centerline. Serving out of bounds or into the net.

Player Positions

3 players at the front (near net): blockers and spikers. 3 players at the back: defensive players and servers. Libero: a special back-row player (wears a different jersey, cannot spike or serve in some leagues).

Winning the Game

A match is usually best of 5 sets. To win a set: Get 25 points (must lead by at least 2 points). In the 5th set (if needed), teams play to 15 points.

Serving

Start the rally by sending the ball over the net. Types: Underhand Serve: Easy for beginners; swing arm from below. Overhand Serve: More powerful; toss and hit the ball above your head. Jump Serve: Advanced; jump and hit with power and spin.

Passing (Bumping)

Receive the serve or attack and direct the ball to the setter. How: Use your forearms (with arms straight and hands together) to bump the ball up. Key Tip: Keep your body low and arms firm.

Setting

Set up the ball for a teammate to spike. How: Use fingertips (not palms) to push the ball upward. Key Tip: Make a triangle shape with your hands and push with your legs and arms.

Spiking (Attacking)

Purpose: Hit the ball forcefully over the net to score. How: Jump and use an overhead swing to hit the ball downwards. Key Tip: Use power and aim to hit into open areas of the opponent's court.

Blocking

Purpose: Stop or deflect the opponent's spike at the net. How: Jump with arms straight up to block the ball. Key Tip: Time your jump and keep your hands above the net.
